# Initial Email to Creative Scotland

**To:** enquiries@creativescotland.com
**Subject:** Open Fund enquiry – digital arts / practice-led research project

---

Dear Creative Scotland,

I'm writing to check whether a project I'm developing is a good fit for the Open Fund for Individuals before submitting a full application. Short version: it's an anti-engagement project that builds tiny tools to insert a pause between impulse and action.

**The project:** Drift is practice-led research on attention, boredom, and software design. One example is a CLI tool where you type `drift` and simply wait—no outputs, just a pause. The work combines functional software tools (designed to be used less, not more), critical writing, and participatory workshops. The website is live at [drift.blog](https://drift.blog) with a manifesto and early writing already published.

**What I'm asking for:** £20,000 over 9 months to build the tools, write a series of essays, run 4–6 workshops across Scotland, and engage with Scottish universities working on related questions (digital culture, interaction design, philosophy of technology).

**Background:** I received Creative Scotland Innovation Fund support in 2013 (£75,000) for a creative technology project. I'm a Glasgow-based technologist and writer with 20+ years in creative technologies.

**Why I'm asking:** The project sits between digital arts, critical design, and public engagement. I want to confirm this kind of practice-led work fits the Open Fund before preparing a full application.

I'm happy to share a short summary deck or discuss by phone if helpful.
